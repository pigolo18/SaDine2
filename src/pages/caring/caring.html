Taking Care of Cats


The first thing you want to do is decide whether you want to adopt a kitten or an adult cat. If you want to get a kitten, you better prepare yourself because kittens are mostly playful and energetic. Adult cats however are calmer and quieter but may have behavioural issues due to its early life. So think carefully on what you want.

Ask medical conditions on a specific cat. Always consider the breed of the cat you’re about to choose. Mixed and purebred cats often differ in different genetic problems.

Consider your free time. Cats are independent animals but that doesn’t mean that they don’t need your attention. They’re active pets that need time and commitment and all the love and affection you can give them.

Calculate whether you can afford a cat. Cats in the Philippines usually cost around Php5,000 and above, depending on what breed you’re getting, its age, and whether or not it has its shots done. You’ll also need to prepare for medical fees when your cat gets sick.
